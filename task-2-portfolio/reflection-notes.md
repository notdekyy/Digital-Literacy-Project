# The Platforms I Chose:

For the second module, I have created a professional presence by setting up a **GitHub** account, a **LinkedIn** account, and a **Kaggle** account. These are the essential ‘Digital Storefront’ for any modern engineer, specifically one who is focused on the field of AI and ML. The applications of the same are as follows:

* **GitHub** – I used **GitHub** as a hosting platform for my technical repositories, while also including a custom profile README file that gives information on my academic background and objectives.

* **LinkedIn** – This serves as my primary professional network, where I have outlined my current education in B.Tech at VIT Bhopal to increase visibility to recruitment agents and peers.

* **Kaggle** – Lastly, I chose **Kaggle** as a space to interact with real-world data and participate in global data science competitions which is essential for my technical development.

# How I Plan to Use Them:
Over the next four years, I plan to utilize these platforms as a traceable record of my journey through the engineering process. **GitHub** will be used for version control-based collaboration on all college projects. In addition, I plan to ensure that all code is clean and well-documented. On **LinkedIn**, I plan to network with potential mentors and highlight my achievements as a Student Digital Ambassador to secure summer internships. Simultaneously, **Kaggle** will be used to hone my machine learning skills from beginner-based competitions to hosting datasets of my own. In essence, by utilizing these platforms consistently, I am creating a digital footprint that will help me transition from a student to a professional.
