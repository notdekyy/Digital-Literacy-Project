# Case Study: UPI "Request Money" Fraud

## Definition of the Crime
**UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud** involves the use of various fraudulent activities performed by cyber criminals to steal money from the victim’s account without any kind of authorization. One type of UPI fraud is "Request Money" fraud, in which the attackers trick the victim based on their lack of technical knowledge about "Collect" requests via UPI.

## How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

**1.**  A student, "Arjun," lists a sale for an old textbook in a student marketplace. Within minutes of posting the sale, the buyer contacts Arjun and seems overly eager to buy the textbook. The buyer claims that they wish to make payment for the textbook right away in order to "hold" the textbook.

**2.**  The scammer informs Arjun that they will be sending the money through a UPI app, but they inform him that he needs to "confirm" the transaction on his end for the "business account" transfer to work.

**3.**  The scammer sends a UPI Collect Request for ₹1,500. Arjun receives a notification on his phone that says: "Requesting money from you."

**4.**  Meanwhile, while the victim, Arjun, is looking at the screen, the scammer remains on the call, pressuring the victim to make the payment, saying, "Just click on 'Pay' and enter your PIN to get the money. It’s a secure refund link."

**5.**  In the hurry, Arjun enters his UPI PIN. Instead of receiving the money, ₹1,500 is deducted instantly from Arjun’s account and transferred to the scammer’s account. The scammer hangs up the call and blocks Arjun’s number.

## Target Audience
College students and young adults using digital marketplaces are also common targets, as they are often engaging in multiple small transactions and are easily swayed by the "urgency" of the buyer.

## Consequences
The victims face a direct economic loss, which may range from a few thousand to several lakhs. Apart from this economic loss, it results in severe psychological distress and a loss of confidence in digital financial systems.
