# Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students

* A PIN is only needed to send money or check your balance. If anyone asks you to put in a PIN to "verify a refund," they are a scammer.
* It is best to decline unexpected payment requests via apps such as GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, as this is one way to withdraw funds from your account.
* Make your VITyarthi portal, Gmail, and social media accounts more secure with an extra layer of security, which is beyond the password.
* It is important to make sure that the website's URL begins with 'https://' before entering your registration number and other personal details on the website.
* Never access your bank accounts or enter any password when you are connected to any public open and insecure internet connection.
* The scammers tend to pretend to be university officials or banks to create a sense of urgency. It is always important to check the claims by contacting the college.
* Create complex passwords for different platforms and avoid using easily guessable information like your name and date of birth.
* It is recommended that you regularly check your mobile settings to ensure that apps are only accessing the data they need to work properly.

## How to Report Cybercrime in India
In the event that you or your peer is targeted by an online scam or threat, the official channels to turn to are:

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
* **National Helpline Number: 1930** (Available 24x7)
